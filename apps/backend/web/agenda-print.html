Introduction of Toastmasters

Toastmasters International is a non-profit educational organization that teaches public speaking & leadership skills through a worldwide network of clubs. Founded in 1924 and headquartered in Englewood, Colorado, it now reaches 300,000+ members in 15,800+ clubs across 149 countries, helping people from diverse backgrounds become more confident speakers, communicators, and leaders.

Regular Meeting Process

  1. Prepared Speech Session: members deliver prepared speeches per their manual requirements.
  2. Table Topic Session: members and guests give 1–2 minute impromptu speeches, following A (Answer), R (Reason), E (Explanation), M (Message).
  3. Evaluation Session: individual evaluators and the General Evaluator evaluate prepared speeches and meeting role takers separately.
